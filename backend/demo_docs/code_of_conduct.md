# Acme Corp Code of Conduct

## Purpose and Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all employees, contractors, and interns of Acme Corp, both on company premises and at any work-related event or online channel. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## Expected Behavior
Employees are expected to act with integrity, treat colleagues with respect, and avoid conflicts of interest. Any conflict of interest — such as a financial stake in a vendor or a close personal relationship within a reporting line — must be disclosed to HR within 7 days of becoming aware of it.

## Anti-Harassment Policy
Acme Corp has a zero-tolerance policy toward harassment, including sexual harassment, bullying, and discrimination based on race, gender, religion, age, disability, or sexual orientation.

Complaints may be reported confidentially to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) at icc@acme.example. The ICC must acknowledge a complaint within 48 hours and complete its investigation within 90 days. Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is itself a serious violation of this Code.

## Dress Code
The standard dress code is business casual. On client-facing days and external meetings, formal business attire is required. Fridays are designated smart-casual days. Employees representing the company at conferences should follow the formal business attire standard.

## Use of Company Resources
Company devices and accounts are provided for work purposes. Limited personal use is permitted but must not interfere with work or violate the law. Confidential company information must never be shared outside the company without written authorization. Sharing customer data externally without approval is grounds for immediate termination.

## Disciplinary Process
The disciplinary process follows a graduated structure:
1. **Verbal warning** — documented in the employee's file.
2. **Written warning** — issued for repeated or more serious issues.
3. **Performance improvement plan (PIP)** — typically lasting 60 days.
4. **Termination** — for gross misconduct or failure to improve after a PIP.

Gross misconduct (such as theft, fraud, violence, or a serious data breach) may result in immediate termination without progressing through the earlier steps. Employees have the right to appeal any disciplinary decision to the Head of People within 10 working days.

## Acknowledgment
All employees must sign an acknowledgment of this Code of Conduct within 14 days of joining and re-acknowledge it annually during the compliance review in February.
